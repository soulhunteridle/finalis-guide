Optional — If You Want to Spend a Little

This guide is intentionally written from a fully F2P perspective. However if you're open to a one-time small spend, the Red Diamond Plus Set is the only purchase worth considering at the start:

  • Any purchase unlocks free ads — saves a significant amount of time in idle games
  • The Plus Set gives 1,800 Red Diamonds — enough to buy the Monthly Pension (1,500 RD) which returns 2,200 RD every 14 days. Self-sustaining forever.
  • Don't buy the cheaper Red Diamond Basic Set — only 300 RD, not enough for Pension

If you do spend, here's what's worth buying with Red Diamonds beyond the Pension loop.

  • Both Event Passes — gives event currency, strong value
  • Both Event Step-Ups — only up to 1A event currency each, stop there
  • Otherworld Pass (optional)
  • Territory Pass (optional)
  • Arena Pass (optional)

The two event passes (the ones that give event currency) are highly recommended — event currency gives you options on what to invest in, and both passes together give strong value. The two event step-ups are also worth doing, but only up to 1A event currency reward each — don't go beyond that. The Otherworld Pass, Territory Pass, and Arena Pass are all permanent but optional — they suffer from diminishing returns over time as covered in this guide as you will read.

Everything else in this guide assumes F2P. If you do purchase, the strategy remains the same.

Events

Once unlocked, finish the mini game section first. Get all rewards and use event currency to buy Scythe summon tickets first. At the beginning your smash damage has no elemental boost, making the Orb buff 0 — so Scythe and Deco are the priority. Aim for Legend rarity on both.

Defeat Monster Exchange

Find it to the left of the idle reward chest. Always pick the Treasure Dungeon key. You'll use this later — trust the process.

Ability Roll

Once unlocked for Equipment and Element, use auto roll and aim for Special All Dmg Dealt and Special Opt Atk Power — split between both (Ignore the atk speed on the image). At this early stage, both stats are ability-exclusive so splitting gives better damage than focusing on one. Don't chase Legend or Mythic here — dice is scarce and you'll regret it later.

Skills

Use the lowest cooldown skill to help proc more smash damage. Smash is your main DPS in early game regardless of which job you'll eventually build toward. Once you unlock Lightning Whirlwind, pillar of fire, and swift slash use them.

Hero

Use the lowest cooldown hero to help proc more smash damage. Smash is your main DPS in early game regardless of which job you'll eventually build toward. Once you unlock bathory, natchal, and aurora use them.

Abyssal Equipment

Just use the recommended equip button for now. Enhancement slots will unlock as you progress — don't worry about it yet. You'll be working toward +12/+13 balance in the next phase.

Equipment Pull Order

This is your main progression goal for this phase. Follow this exact order:

  1. Scythe + Deco → Legend 1
  2. Scythe → Mythic 4 (if lucky: Mythic 3, 2, or 1 is better)
  3. Orb → Legend 1
  4. Deco → Mythic 4
  5. Orb → Mythic 4
  6. Scythe → Mythic 3, 2, or 1 (if all 3 are at Mythic 4)

Minimum target after this phase: Scythe Mythic 4, Orb Mythic 4, Deco Mythic 4.

Catalogue

Check it once in a while and claim any uncollected buffs. Do NOT upgrade all your equipment to level 200 just to unlock catalogue buffs — the return is too diminishing.

Guild

Check the leaderboard for the highest ranked guild with an open spot on your server. Search by name and apply. Aim for one with instant join rather than waiting for approval — unless you know a player who can get you into a top active guild. Active guild buffs give a real damage boost.

World Boss

Just get the highest score you can. The Abyssal Flame resource you earn is used for Abyssal Equipment awakening once your enhancement reaches +15.

Abyssal Equipment Enhancement

This is the phase where you balance all pieces to +12 or +13. At Stage 1-5 you were likely only reaching +7 or +8 — now is when you finish that job.

Don't go +14 on everything before going to +15. One piece at +15 gives more buff than all pieces at +14. Work toward +15 one by one, starting with Emblem (rightmost piece) when you're ready in the next phase.

Skill and Hero Dungeon

These give resources to upgrade skills and heroes. Do NOT upgrade your skill or hero yet — max out your passives first. Only use keys here if you need to upgrade passives. Note: passives share the same resource pool as skills, but not heroes.

Stage 10-50 is designed to be the first hard wall. Here's how to break through:

  1. Pull your Crit Damage passive to max breakthrough and highest level possible
  2. Pull some Blue and Purple runes — use only 5 keys per clear. See image below for which runes to target:

  3. Use Abyssal tickets or dungeon keys for higher equipment or higher enhancement level

Don't over-invest here. The 10-50 wall isn't that hard. Just use enough resource to break through — don't dump everything.

Abyssal Equipment — Push to +15 and Start Awakening

Now that all pieces are at +12/+13, it's time to push toward +15. Start with Emblem first (rightmost piece), then the rest one by one. Jump directly from +12/+13 to +15 — don't stop at +14 on everything.

Once Emblem hits +15, this is when you start Awakening — the system that uses Abyssal Flame from World Boss:

  • Aim for at least S tier on top or middle buff on Emblem first
  • For all other equipment, aim for S tier on top buff
  • Awakening tiers go: Brown → Green → Blue → Purple → Yellow → Orange
  • Once all slots are S at current tier, balance up to the next tier together — don't push one piece to next tier alone
  • Never gamble essence if you can't reach the next guaranteed tier threshold — SS at same tier is only around 2x gain with 1.5% chance of happening, not worth burning scarce essence
  • Awakening priority mirrors enhancement: Emblem first, then balance the rest
⚠ Abyssal Flame is scarce. Don't waste it gambling for SS at the same tier.

Ability Roll Adjustment & Sentence Equipment
Hard Boss Battle Unlocks at Stage 45-1

How to access: More → Boss Battle → top left → right arrow next to "Normal" text

Ability Roll — Switch to Pure Opt Atk Power at Character Level 5000

Around this phase (could happen slightly earlier during Stage 16-45 — check your character level), you'll hit level 5000. This is the switching point.

Before level 5000: Split between All Dmg Dealt + Opt Atk Power — both were ability-exclusive early on.

At level 5000+: Switch to pure Opt Atk Power only on all ability sources. All Dmg Dealt now has enough other sources that it's no longer exclusive. Only Opt Atk Power remains the exclusive stat — fill the empty multiplier.

Still fine to stay at Special rarity for now — no need to chase Legend or Mythic yet.

Applies to all sources: Equipment, Attribute Enhancement, Soul Enhancement.

Ability Set Slots Explained

Each ability source has 3 ability set slots — these are saved presets you can switch between, not 3 simultaneously active rolls. Use them strategically:

  • Slot 1 (all jobs): Full Opt Atk Power
  • Slot 2 (Archmage only, optional): Full Atk Speed at least Special — for testing Archmage's attack speed threshold (exact number TBD, will be updated)
  • Slot 3: unused for now

For Sentence equipment specifically:

  • Reaper & Dark Lord — Slot 1: 1× Ancient Opt Atk Power + 1× Ancient Opt Atk Amp (rest Opt Atk Power)
  • Archmage — Slot 1: Same as above. Slot 2: Full Atk Speed (at least Special)

For Sentence ability rolls, still aim for Special Opt Atk Power for now.

Archmage benefits from Attack Speed differently than other jobs. At a certain Opt Atk Power threshold, the damage ratio gained from additional Attack Speed begins to outpace the ratio gained from more Opt Atk Power — meaning Attack Speed becomes the better investment past that point. On top of that, Archmage relies on proccing skills rapidly, so higher Attack Speed directly translates to more skill procs and more consistent DPS. The exact threshold is still being tested and will be updated here when confirmed.
How to Roll Ancient on Sentence (When Ready)

Don't start rolling Ancient on Sentence until at least Legend 4 or 3. My personal start was Legend 2 (Luparug Crest from Hard Boss Battle).

1

Use auto roll. Check both Ancient Opt Atk Power and Ancient Opt Atk Amp. Do NOT check "All option slots auto change".

2

By doing number 1, your First roll will stop once either of the slot gives you Ancient Opt Atk or Ancient Amp.

3

For second roll: only check what you still need. Got Opt Atk first? Only check Opt Atk Amp. Got Amp first? Only check Opt Atk. Do NOT check "All option slots auto change".

4

By doing number 3, you'll have 1 of each opt atk and opt atk amp on Ancient. STOP HERE, Don't fill all slots with Ancient — it makes you dependent on that roll and make future rerolls very expensive on scarce dice.

The 2-Tier Gap Rule

Only roll Ancient again every 2 sentence tiers to preserve dice:

Sentence Tier Roll Ancient?
Special 4-1 No — too early
Legend 4 / 3 ✓ First Ancient roll here
Legend 2 / 1 ✓ Second Ancient roll
Mythic 4 / 3 ✓ Third Ancient roll
Mythic 2 / 1 ✓ Continue pattern

If you start at Legend 3, you can roll again at Legend 1. If you start at Legend 4, you can roll again at Legend 2. Always AT LEAST 2 tiers apart.

Otherworld

As a new player you'll be highly unlikely to attack occupied tiles successfully — stronger players will just eat you. So:

  • For scouting: Aim for the 5/hour hexagon — minimizes chance of getting attacked by staying in the safe range
  • For occupying/attacking: Always target empty tiles while you're still new. If you do attack occupied tiles, always target the one with the highest progress (11/12 hour) — you get half of what they've accumulated

Upgrade amp in this rotating order, 1 level each till all reach level 10:

  1. Deco
  2. Orb / Scythe (interchangeable)
  3. Sentence

Once all reach level 10 (milestone boost every 10 levels), you can upgrade every 5 or 10 levels instead of 1 by 1. Keep the same order.

My mistake: I pushed my Deco to level 4 while everything else was still level 0. It's fine early but getting all to level 1 first is better.

Abyssal Exchange

Buy Abyssal Cards and upgrade your Altar. This hasn't hit diminishing returns even on my main account — just keep going.

Spirit vs Job Level 60 — Community vs My Take

Community recommendation: Aim for Job Level 60 before getting Spirit.

My recommendation (fresh F2P perspective): Get Spirit first before chasing Job Level 60.

The community advice makes sense in context — when Spirit was released, most veteran players were already near Job Level 60 and naturally wanted to hit that milestone first. But from a new player perspective I reach job lv 40 when I hit stage 100, making Job Level 60 requires a lot of keys and is a long journey. I always prefer using what I can improve NOW rather than waiting for LATER.

Just think of it like this, by getting spirit first you gain more damage, more stage push, and more dungeon push (including the material for job advancement) while hoarding keys will only gives you.. Keys..

2nd Job Advancement is not covered in this guide — it cannot be realistically speedrun without spending real money at this time.
